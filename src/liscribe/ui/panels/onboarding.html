Welcome to Liscribe

Record, transcribe, and dictate

Liscribe lets you record meetings (Scribe), transcribe existing audio files (Transcribe), and dictate text into any app (Dictate). All processing happens on your Mac — no data is sent to the cloud.

This short setup will guide you through permissions, downloading a speech model, and a quick practice of each workflow.

Permissions

Required permissions

Grant Microphone to record and transcribe. Accessibility and Input Monitoring are only needed for Dictate (voice-to-text in any app); you can skip them and add them later from Settings.

ℹ️ In System Settings, Liscribe currently appears as Terminal or Python in the privacy list. Look for one of those when granting access below.

Accessibility and Input Monitoring are only needed for Dictate. You can continue without them. If you grant them later, quit liscribe completely and relaunch (e.g. run liscribe in terminal) for them to take effect.

✓ All permissions granted — nice!

Download a speech model

Choose at least one model

Transcription uses a local Whisper model. Download at least one (base is a good balance of speed and accuracy).

Speaker capture (optional)

BlackHole

To capture both your microphone and system audio (e.g. the other person in a call) in Scribe, you need BlackHole — a virtual audio driver. You can skip this and add it later from Settings.
